The GSRS project
The Global Substance Registration System (GSRS) enables efficient and accurate exchange of information on substances through their Unique Ingredient Identifiers (UNIIs) which can be generated at any time in the regulatory life cycle. The GSRS defines substances by standardizing scientific descriptions of ingredients (chemical, protein, nucleic acid, polymer, structurally diverse, or mixture) following the guidelines of ISO 11238 and ISO DTS 19844. See more about GSRS below including information on its registration system, access to public data (including data curated by the FDA), as well as a regulatory workflow .
GSRS was developed through a collaboration between the FDA, NIH's National Center for Advancing Translational Sciences (NCATS), and international partners including EMA, USP, WHO, UMC, BfARM.

Public Data

GSRS is a data set of over 160,000 publicly available substance records. Chemistry, associated metabolites, impurities, diseases, etc.

FDA's GSRS instance enables efficient and accurate exchange of information on substances through their Unique Ingredient Identifiers (UNIIs) which can be generated at any time in the regulatory life cycle. UNII Search System.
Insight Drugs is a comprehensive and curated portal for translational research using the GSRS dataset.
Regulatory Workflow

Based on ISO1128 standard, GSRS is a regulatory workflow providing consistent definitions of substances globally.

PrecisionFDA Is one platform using GSRS's workflow and software to upload, search and register multi-omics data. Community-sourced science such as that done through challenges offered by PrecisionFDA advance regulatory standards for bioinformatics, RWD, and AI
