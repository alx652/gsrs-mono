The GSRS project
The Global Substance Registration System (GSRS) enables efficient and accurate exchange of information on substances through their Unique Ingredient Identifiers (UNIIs) which can be generated at any time in the regulatory life cycle. The GSRS defines substances by standardizing scientific descriptions of ingredients (chemical, protein, nucleic acid, polymer, structurally diverse, or mixture) following the guidelines of ISO 11238 and ISO DTS 19844. See more about GSRS below including information on its registration system, access to public data (including data curated by the FDA), as well as a regulatory workflow.
GSRS was developed through a collaboration between the FDA, NIH's National Center for Advancing Translational Sciences (NCATS), and international partners including EMA, USP, UMC, BfARM.
